Framing

From the perspective of target users, this project is aimed at individuals who experience information overload, especially those who spend long periods in high-intensity digital environments. These users may not be familiar with complex creative and therefore require clear information guidance and simple interaction methods to decrease the barrier to use.

In terms of context of use, this tool is more suitable for short and personal moments, such as in situations where users seek a moment of quiet. It does not attempt to continuously occupy the user’s attention and time. It is also not intended to function as a productivity tool.

In terms of scope, this project deliberately limits itself to a single core interaction, which is to visualise breathing through user input. This limitation is an intentional design decision aimed at avoiding complexity, maintaining a meditative experience, and allowing users to concentrate on their internal state rather than being distracted by multiple functions.

In terms of visual form, I chose to use concentric circles as the main element, mainly based on two considerations. First, the circles resemble the expanding form of water ripples. When people encounter water, they often experience a sense of healing and calm. Introducing this visual imagery into the design helps to reinforce the peaceful atmosphere of the work. Secondly, the repetition and layering of circles may also remind people of tree rings, thereby delivering a sense of the passage of time. As the circles are generated and change one by one, users can visually perceive a process of continuous accumulation, which give a connection with the flow of time. Conceptually, this visual form is combined with breathing because meditation usually begins with focusing on one’s own breath. I believe that this attention to breathing can help alleviate psychological states such as anxiety. Therefore, I aim to bring this experience into the project, allowing users through the combination of breathing and visual changes to gradually enter a more relaxed state.

WILD HEDGE NEST

Three in-class experiments provided me with many insights. Firstly, in the information experiment, I realised that effective information presentation can significantly improve user experience. Information is not limited to text, icons and visual elements can also serve as important direction. In addition, the visibility and clarity of text directly affect how well users understand the system. Poor information presentation can lead to confusion and hesitation. In my project, I will use textual guidance to ensure clear information direction. Secondly, in the feedback experiment, I came to understand that feedback is an essential part of any interaction. Every user action should be responded to by the system. Without feedback, interaction becomes uncertain. Therefore, this project emphasises immediate visual feedback to ensure that users can clearly perceive the results of their actions. Thirdly, the mapping experiment was the most difficult for me to fully understand. I gradually came to understand mapping as the relationship between user actions and system outcomes. Although I have developed a general understanding of this concept, I will not focus too much on it in this project.

In addition to these experiments, I also analysed some existing creative tools, such as Intersymmetric. While I appreciate its clean and consistent visual style and typography, I found that its information design is not very clear. When first entering the interface, it is difficult to understand what actions should be taken. Some elements appear to be text but are actually links, and the system provides very limited guidance. This makes it difficult to use, especially for users who are not familiar with music creative tools. From this, I realised that even in minimal website, clarity of information must be maintained. I also explored Concrete Nest. and Musical Text Editor. I find these tools interesting because most creative tools tend to rely on images or sound, while these tools that use text as the main medium. Although the idea of using text to carry creative expression is appealing, I was not able to come up with many interesting ideas. Therefore, I decided not to use text as the main interaction method in my project.

Another important consideration is the controllability among creative tools. Some tools allow users to fully control the outcome, producing results that closely match user expectations. While others generate results that are more difficult to predict. Even when users can adjust parameters, the outcome cannot be fully anticipated. For example, in blobSketch although users can adjust certain values, the final visual result still feels somewhat uncontrollable. Similarly, in Grid Paint even simple operations such as “+” or “–” can lead to unexpected changes, because the system does not clearly explain how these inputs affect the outcome. This lack of transparency makes it difficult for users to achieve their intended results. In my project, I aim to explore a balance between controllability and unpredictability. I want users to feel that their actions meaningfully influence the outcome, while still allowing unexpected visual results.

Purpose

This project aims to create an interactive experience that allows users to visualise their breathing through direct interaction. In this process, users generate their own breathing tracks. These tracks are not pre-defined, but are gradually formed through interaction, allowing users to externalise a process that originally belongs inside the body into a visible form.

When the user presses and holds the mouse, it represents breath in. At the same time, concentric circles are generated outward from the cursor position. When the user releases the mouse and hovers, the breath in ends and the breath out begins. The generation of the circles stops, and each circle gradually disperses into evenly distributed dots. During this breath out process, the dots become more loosely arranged over time. Users can freely initiate each "breath" anywhere on the canvas we have set up. Users are also able to download the breathing tracks they have created.

This interaction is intended to make breathing which is a process that is usually unconscious become visible and perceptible. At the same time, as users interact, they will gradually notice the slow passage of time and the subtle rhythms of the body. Therefore, this tool is not only a creative tool, but also a way of guiding attention.

Worth

In today’s digital society, people are constantly exposed to information overload. In such an environment, it is becoming increasingly difficult for people to stop and become aware of their internal state. As a result, psychological issues are becoming more common, and most people experience feelings of irritation and anxiety to some extent.

This project provides a peaceful space where users can temporarily step away from this state of information overload. Instead of continuing to receive information, users interact with their own breathing and create something that belongs to and represents themselves. By constructing their own breathing track, users may better understand their emotional state. This tool does not deliver content but invites users to feel themselves. It also allows users to relax through repeated breathing and feel a moment that belongs only to them.

In this sense, its value lies in creating a space for introspection, where users can find a quiet state and reconnect with their own feelings.
